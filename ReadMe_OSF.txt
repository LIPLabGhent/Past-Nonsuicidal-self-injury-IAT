## Name/identifier study:			Predicting Nonsuicidal Self-Injury Using a Variant of the Implicit Association Test

## Authors:					Femke Cathelyn, Pieter Van Dessel, & Jan De Houwer 

## Date:					13/08/2021 

## Reference of the publication:		Cathelyn, F., Van Dessel, P., & De Houwer, J. (2021). Predicting nonsuicidal self-injury using a variant of the implicit association test. Suicide and Life-Threatening Behavior. 

=========================================================================================================

1. Purpose of the project: Test the predictive utility of a variant of the Implicit Association Test (IAT), the Past Nonsuicidal Self-Injury IAT (P-NSSI-IAT), for NSSI in online samples. 

2. Description Studies 
 
2a. Description Study 1: Validate the P-NSSI-IAT by assessing its ability to detect prior NSSI behavior.

2b. Description Study 2: Test whether results from Study 1 could be replicated while at the same time investigating the ability of the P-NSSI-IAT to prospectively predict NSSI behavior.  

3. Open Science Framework: https://osf.io/wnbfc/

4. Current status of the manuscript: in press 

5. Ethics: The ethical committee of the Faculty of Psychology and Educational Sciences at Ghent University approved this project (approval number 2020/72 Femke Cathelyn). The research was carried out in accordance with the provisions of the World Medical Association Declaration of Helsinki. Informed consent was obtained from all participants included in the studies.
